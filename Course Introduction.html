Design and Analysis of Algorithms

The Design and Analysis of Algorithms course dives into the fundamental concepts and techniques used to develop efficient, robust algorithms. This course covers key topics including:

  • Introduction to Algorithms: Understanding basic algorithms and their applications.
  • Complexity Analysis: Learning how to evaluate the time and space complexity of algorithms.
  • Divide and Conquer: Exploring techniques to break problems into smaller subproblems and solve them recursively.
  • Greedy Algorithms: Studying algorithms that make the best local choice at each stage to find an optimal global solution.
  • Dynamic Programming: Learning methods to solve problems by breaking them down into simpler subproblems and storing their solutions.
  • Graph Algorithms: Delving into algorithms related to graph structures such as shortest paths, minimum spanning trees, and network flow.
  • Sorting and Searching: Examining classic algorithms for sorting and searching data efficiently.
  • NP-Completeness: Understanding the classification of problems based on their computational difficulty.

Objectives:

  • Develop a deep understanding of various algorithmic techniques.
  • Learn to analyze and compare algorithm efficiencies.
  • Apply algorithms to solve real-world problems.
  • Gain insights into advanced topics like NP-Completeness and approximation algorithms.

Prerequisites:

  • Basic knowledge of data structures.
  • Familiarity with programming languages like C++ or Java.

This course equips students with the tools and knowledge needed to design and analyze algorithms effectively, preparing them for more advanced studies and professional challenges in computer science.
